A field guide
Cosmic Scale
From the Moon to the edge of the observable universe
Scene I — Earth & Moon
If Earth were a basketball, the Moon would be a tennis ball
held at arm's length — about 7 metres away.
Scene II — Earth & Sun
Light leaving the Sun takes 8 minutes and 20 seconds
to reach Earth. We see the Sun as it was 8 minutes ago.
Scene III — Speed of Light
In one second, light travels 299,792 km —
enough to circle Earth 7.5 times.
Scene IV — Jupiter
All other planets in the Solar System could fit inside Jupiter
with room to spare. It contains 1,300 Earths by volume.
Scene V — The Milky Way
The Milky Way contains 200–400 billion stars.
Our Sun is an ordinary star about 26,000 light-years from the galactic centre.
End of field guide
